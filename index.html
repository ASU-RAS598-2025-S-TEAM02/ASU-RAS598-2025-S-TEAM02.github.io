Project Plan

Introduction

Our project seeks to answer the research question: How can we develop autonomous robotic systems that effectively integrate sensor data to perform complex tasks in dynamic environments?

Sensor Integration

We will utilize sensor data collected from the robot to enhance its decision-making capabilities. During testing, sensors will be used to gather data on the robot's performance and environment. In the final demonstration, sensors will enable the robot to adapt to changing conditions and perform tasks autonomously.

Interaction

We plan to influence the behavior of our robot through a combination of user interfaces and automated control systems. We will develop a web-based interface for monitoring and controlling the robot, as well as a data storage system for logging sensor data and performance metrics.

Control and Autonomy

Our control system will integrate feedback from sensors to make high-level decisions and adjust the robot's actions in real-time. This will involve developing algorithms for sensor fusion, path planning, and obstacle avoidance.

Preparation Needs

To be successful, we need to deepen our understanding of sensor integration, control algorithms, and user interface design. We would benefit from additional lectures and resources on these topics in class.

Final Demonstration

For our final demonstration, we will showcase the robot's ability to perform tasks autonomously in a controlled environment. We will need access to a testing area, power supplies, and network connectivity. Our robot will handle environmental variability through adaptive algorithms and robust sensor integration. We will verify our system's performance through extensive testing and evaluation.

Impact

This project will drive us to learn new material and contribute to course development by exploring advanced topics in robotics. It will encourage learning in areas such as sensor integration, control systems, and user interface design.

Advising

Our project advisor will be Dr. Aukes, who will provide mentoring and access to specialized hardware and resources. Dr. Aukes is willing to participate and expects regular progress updates and demonstrations of our work.
